v5.32.0-rc1 — selection, scrollback, prompt-aware tap

Drag-select extends past the top/bottom of the viewport into
scrollback (#94). The viewport scrolls under your finger and the
selection's anchor stays anchored on the line you originally
pressed.

Soft-wrap rejoin on copy now consults libvterm's authoritative
softWrapped flag instead of a column-length heuristic. Wrapped
long commands paste back without spurious mid-line newlines, and
hand-typed lines that happen to fill the viewport no longer get
joined with the next line.

Settings → Terminal → Scrollback rows lets you bump per-tab
history beyond the previous compile-time 1000 (#151). Presets
1 000 / 5 000 / 10 000 / 25 000; description spells out the
memory cost so you opt in deliberately.

New Settings → Terminal toggle "Tap to move cursor on supported
prompts" — synthesises arrow-key dispatches when you tap inside
the active prompt's input region. Requires OSC 133 prompt
markers (starship, fish 3.6+, recent bash/zsh shell-integration
setups). No effect on shells that don't emit them. Default off.
